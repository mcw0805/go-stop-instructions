The Game of Go-Stop

Scoring

This section explains how to calculate the points.

Calculating Base Score

Card Score Distribution

  • A combination of at least 3 kwang cards extra adds the points values described below.

Yeol cards are calculated as follows:

  • A set of 5 yeol cards scores 1 point.
  • Each additional yeol card adds 1 extra point.
  • Special combination adds extra points (as described below).

Pi cards are calculated as follows:

  • A set of 10 pi cards scores 1 point.
  • Each additional pi card adds 1 extra point.
  • Each additional ssangpi card adds 2 extra points.

Calling "Go"
  • Each call of “Go” adds 1 point.
  • Staring with 3-Go, the total score multiplied by a factor of 2.
    • 3-Go: 2x current score.
    • 4-Go: 4x current score.
    • 5-Go: 8x current score.
    • etc.
Calculating Multiplying Factor

The player's score can be multiplied by some factor of 2 if one or more bak occurs.

After a player's winning, bak is an event for which the opponent players are penalized.

  • Condition:
    • The winning player has collected effectively at least 10 pi cards (i.e. they scored at least 1 point just with pi cards).
    • The losing player has less than or equal to 5 pi cards in their collection.
  • How to Escape Penalty: If the winning player calls "Go," the losing player will have a chance to escape from the penalty by collecting more pi cards.
  • Condition:
    • The winning player has collected effectively at least 3 kwang cards (i.e. they scored at least 2 points just with kwang cards)
    • The losing player has zero cards in their collection.
  • How to Escape Penalty: If the winning player calls "Go," the losing player will have a chance to escape from the penalty by at least one kwang card.
  • Condition:
    • The winning player has at least 7 yeol cards in their collection.
    • Note: Opponent player is NOT penalized for not having any yeol cards.
  • How to Escape Penalty: The opponent player cannot escape the penalty.
  • Condition:
    • Another player has already called "Go," but the current player scores the minimum required scores to win and calls stop.
  • How to Escape Penalty: If points of the winning player and the opponent players are too close, it may be better to call "Stop" and move onto the next round.

Special Notes:

  • Two players: The penalty is twice the score of the final winning player.
  • Three players: If the new winning player calls "Stop", the first player who called "Go" has to cover for the third players losses in addition to their own penalties.


Other Events That Contribute to Multiplying Factors
  • Placing a bomb.
  • Shaking: the player must have had their 3 cards revealed to all the players in the game.

Special Notes:

  • Multiple bombs/shakes are also cumulative. For example, two bombs will lead to a multiplying factor of 4.

Final Score Breakdown
  • Calculating Base Points
    • Kwang: 2 points
      • 3 kwang cards with December kwang = 2 points
    • Yeol: 8 points
      • Godori = 5 points
      • 7 yeol cards = 3 points
        • 5 yeol cards = 1 point
        • 2 additional yeol cards = 2 point
    • Tti: 4 points
      • Chodan = 3 points
      • 5 yeol cards = 1 points
    • Pi: 5 points
      • (Effectively) 10 pi cards = 1 point
      • (Effectively) 4 pi cards = 4 points
  • Calculating Multiplying Factor
    • multiplying Factor: 4
      • Mung-bak = 2 x base score
      • Kwang-bak (since the current player's kwang's contributed to their score AND the opponent player does not have any kwang cards in their collection) = 2 x base score

Total points = (kwang points + yeol points + tti points + pi points) x multiplying factor

    = (2 + 8 + 4 + 5) x 4 = 19 x 4 = 76 points

What if the current player has called total of 5 Go's?

Answer: Then 5 points will be added to their base score and this score would multiply by another factor 8, becoming (19 + 5) x [4 (from bak) x 8 (from 5 Go)] = 24 x 32 = 768 points
