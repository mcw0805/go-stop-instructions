The Game of Go-Stop

Go-Stop

Tired and bored of the typical poker, blackjack, and UNO?

If you are, you should try the traditional Korean game of Go-Stop.
Short spurts of rounds with potentially exponential amount of winnings going back and forth!


Go-Stop is a traditional Korean card game played with a with special set of cards that originated from Japan called hanafuda (花札), which translates to “flower cards.” In Korean, hanafuda is known as hwatu (화투).

The game consists of matching cards and earning points. The players make decisions in each turn and set of special events that happen by chance make the game exciting!

Game Type: Card Game

Recommended Age: 18+

Players: 2-6

Required: Deck of hwatu
