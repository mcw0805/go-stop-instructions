The Game of Go-Stop

Additional Rules & 3+ Player Rules

Additional Set of Rules

Many players decide to add additional set of rules to make the game more exciting. These may vary depending on the region or platform. Some of them are listed below.

  • First Ppuk, Jjok, and Ttadak: If a player performs any of these events on their first turn of the play, they are awarded a certain amount of money that the players agree on.
  • Department Store: If a player collects a combination of kwang, yeol, tti, and pi, they are awarded a certain amount of money that the players agree on.
  • Last Ttadak, Jjok, Sseul, and Collection of Ppuk: If a player performs any of these events with their last card in their hands, they cannot extort their opponent's pi cards.
  • Last Ppuk: If a player makes a ppuk with their last card in their hands, it is not actually counted. The player may leave the flipped card on the floor.
Playing With More Than 3 Players

It is theoretically possible to include 4 or more players in the game round, but only three players can directly participate.

The rule for 4 or more players is called kwang-pal-ee (광팔이) because meaning "kwang seller."

  • Dealing cards is similar to dealing to 3 players: 4 cards to each player, 3 to the floor, 3 to each player and 3 to the floor (See the Game Setup section for more details). The dealer has to deal the floor cards face down.
  • The dealer has to be one of the three who must participate in the game.
  • All other players take a look at their hand cards. The second player (i.e. player immediately right of the dealer) decides to stay in the game or fold, and the next player decides, and so on in counterclockwise direction.
  • As soon as two players decide that they want to continue playing, the remainder players may be forced to drop out. If the player who come earlier in a turn drops out, the last players may be forced to play.
  • The players who are forced drop out are compensated if they hold any of the following cards:
    • Kwang
    • Ssanpi, including September yeol
    • Joker
    • All three of chungdan, hongdan, or chodan combination
    • All three of godori cards
  • The players who drop out voluntarily are not compensated. The dealer does not have to pay for any compensations.

The players should agree on the compensation amount before starting the game. Each card is usually one point worth of amount for each of the cards.
