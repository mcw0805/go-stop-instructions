The Game of Go-Stop

Gameplay

The goal is to maximze the number of points, which are described in detail in the scoring section below. To maxmize the number of points, the player needs to collect the cards. How a player collects cards is described immediately below.


EXAMINING

  • Player examines their hand cards and floor cards in order to see any of the match up.
  • Player may have three of the same month cards in their hand. This is known as a bomb. (Handling bomb described in next step)
  • [Optional] With joker: In case of a joker on hand, the player can place down the joker on their pi pile and take a random card from anywhere in the remaining deck.

MATCHING OR DISCARDING

  • Player attempts to match one of the floor cards with the same month card in their hand.
    1. If the player can match multiple, they must select one. In case of a match with a bomb, the player may place down all three to the floor.
      2. OR
    2. If the player has no matching cards, the player must discard one of them. If the player holds a bomb, the player can decide to shake.

FLIPPING

  • After matching or discarding, the player flips the top card from the remaining deck.
  • [Optional] With joker: In case of a joker, the player flips the top card again from the remaining deck.

COLLECTING

  • Check for any special events (listed below).
  • If there’s a match from the flipped card and the floor card, the player collects those pair of cards along with the matching card pair from their hand (if any) from the first step. Otherwise, the flipped card remains on the floor.
  • [Optional] With joker: If a joker was flipped, the player will also take their joker to their collection pile.

(SOMTIMES) EXTORTING
  • The player may extort one or more of the opponent's pi cards if one or a combination of the below special events occur:
    • After placing a bomb
    • Collecting ppuk
    • Collecting ja-ppuk
    • Sseul
    • Ttadak
    • Jjok

    Special Notes:

    • If a combination of above events to occur, the number of cards to extort will accumulate.
    • If the opponent player does not have no or too few pi cards, the current player will only extort however many pi cards that the opponent has at the time.
    • There is no sense of giving "change." If the current player is to extort one pi but if the opponent has one ssangpi, the current player will still extort the ssangpi without giving one pi back.

  • (SOMETIMES) CALLING "GO" OR "STOP"
    • If the player reaches the required minimum number of points, then they can take one of the following actions
      1. Call "Go": The player decides to continue the game, allowing the next player to execute their turn.
      2. Call "Stop": The player decides to end the game. Score is calculated and winnings are collected. The winning player becomes the dealer of the next game.

      Special Notes:

      • Upon winning, calling "Go" is the only way to increase the winnings for the current game, but it is risky in that the other players may steal the victory.

    Calling "Go"
    • Each call of “Go” adds 1 point.
    • Staring with 3-Go, the total score multiplied by a factor of 2.
      • 3-Go: 2x current score.
      • 4-Go: 4x current score.
      • 5-Go: 8x current score.
      • etc.
    Calculating Multiplying Factor

    The player's score can be multiplied by some factor of 2 if one or more bak occurs.

    After a player's winning, bak is an event for which the opponent players are penalized based on their collected cards and/or other special events that occur during the game.


    Other Events That Contribute to Multiplying Factors
    • Placing a bomb
    • Shaking: the player must have had their 3 cards revealed to all the players in the game.

    Special Notes:

    • Multiple bombs/shakes are also cumulative. For example, two bombs will lead to a multiplicative factor of 4.
